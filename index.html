Section 01

Mansfield is on the rise.

What's taking shape in Mansfield isn't imported. It grows from the torn fibers of what this city has always been. Slow and intentional. Rebuilt in its own image. Fully Mansfield, in identity and soul.

For a long time, the story of Richland County was one of departure. The industries that anchored this city left, and the population followed. What was left was a place that remembered its past, but not yet sure of its future.

But something has been shifting.

Downtown Mansfield feels alive again. For the first time in a long time, it feels like Mansfield. And that matters, because not that long ago, it didn't.

What is taking shape here isn't just for a select few. It's for all who live here. Progress is being driven by those who stayed, those who returned, and a shared commitment to the future of this community. We're building something that reflects who this place actually is, shaped by the people who stayed, by those who came back, and by what has always been here.

And it's already underway.

New leadership is naming a future with clarity. People who stayed and returned are rebuilding what it means for this city to function and belong. The investment going into Mansfield right now is real, and it's adding up.

The decisions being made now will define this city for the next generation.

And in moments like this, certain institutions carry a weight beyond what they do. They signal something deeper. That this city is worth building. That overlooked neighborhoods are part of the story. That what's being rebuilt is meant for everyone.

Because in Mansfield, generosity doesn't disappear. It multiplies.

Section 03

The mission is growing. The building isn't.

The calling for Grace to this work is not in question. For more than 25 years, the pantry has shown up, week after week, for the people of Mansfield. The relationships are there. The trust is there. The need is consistent, visible, and growing.

What's in question now is whether the building can carry what the mission has become.

The constraint is not the mission. It's the building.

Current limitations:

  • No dedicated receiving dock. Trucks arrive with large-scale deliveries, but there is no proper receiving infrastructure.
  • Inability to accept all available food. Volunteers unload thousands of pounds of food by hand through spaces not designed for that volume.
  • Insufficient cold storage. Cold storage capacity is insufficient for the volume of fresh and perishable food available through partners.
  • Limited distribution space. The current distribution space limits the ability to provide a dignified, choice-based experience for guests.

The gap between capacity and demand is widening.

Supply is increasing. Partnerships are expanding. More individuals are being served each year, and demand continues to rise.

The mission is growing. The building is not.

Section 04

Three truths.

The case for this investment doesn't require a long list of statistics. Three facts are enough.

  • 01
    Food insecurity in childhood isn't something children recover from.

    The brain requires roughly 20% of the body's daily energy to develop normally. When that energy is consistently insufficient, the effects on attention, memory, and cognitive development are measurable and lasting. The families who come to Grace aren't just facing short-term need. Their children are in a window of development that doesn't reopen. Meeting that need today creates the stability required for long-term growth tomorrow.

  • 02
    The food that could reach families in this region often doesn't, not because it isn't there.

    In Richland County alone, nearly 1 million pounds of food go to waste each year, even as families across Mansfield go without. The problem isn't supply. It is infrastructure: the ability to receive, store, and distribute food safely, at scale, and with dignity determines whether that food reaches a family or is lost. Grace is already positioned to be that bridge. With a covered receiving dock and expanded cold storage, Grace can accept full semi-truck deliveries and significantly increase the perishable food it captures and distributes. Right now, the building is the gap between that food and the families who need it, and between short-term relief and longer-term stability.

  • 03
    Demand is growing at a pace the current facility can't match.

    The pantry served nearly 50,000 individuals in 2025, a 25% increase over the prior year, and projects another 25% increase ahead. Without this investment, capacity will continue to fall behind rising demand. This is not a future problem. It is the current reality. For many households, access to consistent food is what creates the margin needed to pursue employment, education, and long-term self-sufficiency.

Section 05

What Grace is building.

This renovation does not change the mission. It allows the existing work to operate at full capacity. The undercroft will be reconfigured to match both the scale of the need and the standard of care Grace provides. The pantry served nearly 50,000 individuals in 2025 and projects continued 25% annual growth. This investment equips the facility to meet current demand and sustain future growth without ongoing structural limitations.

Planned improvements include:

Dignified, choice-based distribution space

Redesigned space allows guests to move freely, select what they need, and be served in an environment built around dignity and choice.

Dedicated receiving dock with lift access

A covered dock with scissor lift enables safe, efficient unloading of full semi-truck deliveries.

Expanded cold storage capacity

Walk-in coolers and freezers increase capacity for fresh and perishable food, reducing loss and improving nutritional access.

Dedicated partner service areas

On-site space for health and social service partners enables coordinated support during a single visit.

Outdoor gathering and community space

Green space and a covered pavilion create opportunities for community connection and neighborhood engagement.

Commercial kitchen and improved dry storage

Expanded kitchen and optimized storage improve operational efficiency and support increased volume.

Section 06

What this investment means for Mansfield.

For those who have invested in Mansfield, the question is not only "does this help people?" It is: does this move the city forward?

This investment does both.

Grace functions as essential community infrastructure. Other investments in education, workforce development, and health become more effective. A stable city requires a stable foundation. Grace helps provide it.

When food insecurity rises, the strain shows up elsewhere. In hospitals, social services, and emergency systems. When it is reduced, the strain decreases. A pantry operating at full capacity, alongside its partners, does more than serve individuals. It stabilizes the systems those individuals rely on.

This investment sends a clear signal. Where a community directs resources, especially toward overlooked neighborhoods, reflects what it values. Grace embodies that choice clearly: Mansfield is being rebuilt with its most vulnerable residents, not around them.

Half of this campaign is an endowment. This is intentional. $1 million of the $2.5 million goal is designated to cover operations, staffing, and maintenance over time, not just construction. This reduces reliance on year-to-year fundraising. It allows the pantry to staff appropriately, absorb fluctuations, and serve future generations with consistency. For donors focused on long-term impact, this is a hallmark investment.

Mansfield has always been more than an economy. It's a community. Grace has made that case, daily, for 150 years.

Section 09

The invitation.

What is happening at Grace is not new. It has been built over time, through consistency, through presence, and through a daily commitment to meet people with dignity. This work was in motion long before this campaign, and will continue because it's rooted in something deeper than any single initiative.

The Grace Still Multiplies campaign does not begin new work. It is an invitation to strengthen and extend the work that is already being done, building the infrastructure needed to operate at full capacity and serve the next generation of Mansfield residents with consistency and care.

The impact of this investment may not be immediate or easily measured. Over time, it appears in children whose development stayed consistent, in families who found stability when they needed it most, and in neighborhoods strengthened by reliable support. These outcomes don't fit neatly into a report, but they shape a city over time.

The work is already moving. There is a place for you in it. We invite you to be part of what comes next.
